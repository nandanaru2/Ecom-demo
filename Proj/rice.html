RICE

There are more than 40,000 varieties of cultivated rice (the grass species Oryza sativa) said to exist. But the exact figure is uncertain. Over 90,000 samples of cultivated rice and wild species are stored at the International Rice Gene Bank and these are used by researchers all over the world.

Long Grain

Long Grain

All-purpose long grain rices are imported mainly from the India, USA, Italy, Spain, Surinam, Guyana and Thailand and can be used for all styles of cooking.

Regular Long Grain

Regular Long Grain

One of the most popular types of rice because it has a subtle flavour which perfectly complements both rich and delicate sauces. Milled to remove the husk and bran layer, the grain is slim and 4-5 times as long as it is wide.

Brown Long Grain

Brown Long Grain

This rice has a distinctly nutty flavour. Brown Rice undergoes only minimal milling, which removes the husk but retains the bran layer. Due to this the rice retains more vitamin , mineral and fibre content than regular or easy cook white rice.

Jasmine Rice

Jasmine Rice

Another aromatic rice, although its flavour is slightly less pronounced than basmati. It originates from Thailand. The length and slenderness of the grains suggest that they should remain separate on cooking but it differs from other long grain rices in that it has a soft and slightly sticky texture when cooked.

Japonica

Japonica

Short and medium grain. Grown mainly in California. It comes in a variety of colours including red, brown and black. Its used in Japanese and Caribbean cuisines due to its characteristic clingy moist and firm nature when cooked.
